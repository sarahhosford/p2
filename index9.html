1919

After the right to vote had been granted to women and New Zealand, the Women’s Parliamentary Rights Act was passed with gave women the right to stand for Parliament. Three women contested seats at the 1919 general election, but none were successful until 1933 when The Labour Party’s Elizabeth McCombs became the first female Member of Parliament, winning a by-election in the Lyttelton seat.
