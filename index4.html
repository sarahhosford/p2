1869

‘An appeal to the men of New Zealand’ is thought to be the first written document advocating the women’s right to vote. It was written by Mary Anne Muller in 1888, under the pseudonym ‘Femmina’ so that her husband, who opposed her views, would not know it was her. Muller’s efforts in the suffragette movement were recognised by the leader, Kate Sheppard, and she is now nationally recognised as a main contributor in gain New Zealand’s woman political rights.
